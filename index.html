Teja S17 Dried Red Chilli

Teja chilli is one of the hottest varieties available, predominantly grown in the southern states of India. Its global popularity is due to the fertile soil and proximity to the Guntur Mirchi Market. Widely known as S17 Teja chilli, it is highly favored by international buyers and is exported in stemless, with stem, and powdered forms. Teja chilli is perfect for use in soups, stir-fries, stews, and spice blends.

334 S4/Sannam Dried Red Chilli

The Guntur Sannam chilli is a premium variety known for its vibrant red color and moderate heat, making it perfect for adding both flavor and color to any dish. Grown in the fertile soils of Guntur, Khammam, and Prakasam districts of Andhra Pradesh, this chilli is globally renowned, especially for its consistent quality. Its thick skin ensures a rich, robust flavor when used in powder or crushed form. High in vitamin C and protein, the Guntur Sannam chilli is ideal for health-conscious consumers looking for both taste and nutrition. Widely referred to as S4 Sannam chilli is a top choice for international buyers and perfect for use in spice blends, curries, and sauces. The S4 variety is characterized by its thick red skin, and when crushed, it remains thick, red, and hot.

Bydgi Dried Red Chilli

The Byadgi chilli is a premium chilli variety, prized for its vibrant red color and mild heat. Known for its rich flavor and low pungency, it’s perfect for those who want a deep red hue in their dishes without overwhelming spiciness. Grown in the fertile regions of Karnataka, Byadgi chillies are widely used in making chilli powder, spice blends, and curry pastes. Their smooth texture and natural oils make them an ideal choice for adding flavor and color to sauces, gravies, and seasonings. A top choice for both culinary professionals and home chefs, Byadgi chillies are a must-have in your kitchen!. Byadagi Chilli is physically longer in size, brighter red in colour, full of wrinkles and less spicy. It is widely used in Cuisines and nail polish industry by extracting color from Bydgi Chilli.
